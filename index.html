Advanced Chemical Solutions for FPD & Semiconductor Manufacturing

This document details cutting-edge chemical materials and their applications in advanced display (FPD) and semiconductor fabrication processes. We explore the science behind these materials, focusing on performance characteristics and practical applications.

Display Technologies

Aqueous Strippers for Display Manufacturing

Aqueous-based strippers represent a significant advancement in display fabrication, offering an environmentally conscious alternative to traditional solvent-based solutions. These strippers are formulated to effectively remove photoresist and other polymeric residues from display panels without damaging underlying sensitive layers.

Target Applications

Primarily used in the manufacturing of TFT-LCD and OLED displays for the removal of photoresist materials after patterning processes. Also applicable in the cleaning of photomasks and other components.

Key Advantages of Aqueous Strippers:

  • Environmentally Friendly: Reduced VOC (Volatile Organic Compound) emissions.
  • High Efficiency: Complete removal of various types of photoresist.
  • Material Compatibility: Designed to be compatible with various substrate materials (glass, ITO, metals).
  • Cost-Effective: Often lower overall process cost due to reduced waste disposal and handling requirements.
  • Safety: Improved worker safety due to reduced flammability and toxicity.

LTOC (Low-Temperature Organic Compound) for OLED

LTOC materials are specialized organic compounds designed for use in OLED (Organic Light-Emitting Diode) display fabrication. They play a crucial role in forming thin-film layers with specific optical and electrical properties, enabling high-performance OLED devices.

Key Properties & Performance Metrics

  • High Transparency: Transmission rate (T%) of 98% at 400nm wavelength.
  • Precise Resolution: Achieves a resolution of 10µm Line & Space (L&S).
  • Rapid Photo-Speed: Photo-speed of 90mJ/cm².
  • Controlled Mask C.D.: Precise control over critical dimensions.
  • Optimized Taper Angle: Ensures proper layer formation and device performance.
  • Well-Defined Contact Holes: Facilitates reliable electrical connections.

Target Applications

Touch Panel for Flexible OLED, Low-temperature curing (less than 100℃), OLED Pixel Defined Layer (PDL).

Black Resist for OLED

Black resist materials are essential for creating high-contrast OLED displays. They act as light-blocking layers, preventing light leakage and improving the overall image quality.

Key Properties:

  • High Optical Density (O.D): O.D = 1.0/µm.
  • Standard Developer Compatibility: 2.38% TMAH Development.
  • Fine Resolution: 14µm Hole resolution.
  • Two Types: Cardo-based and Siloxane-based black resists are available.

Target Applications

Light blocking, Anti-reflection, Pixel Define Layer, R, G, B Pattern.

Photo-sensitive Polyimide (PSPI)

Photo-sensitive polyimides (PSPIs) are advanced materials used in microelectronics for their excellent thermal stability, chemical resistance, and dielectric properties. They combine the benefits of polyimides with the ability to be patterned using photolithography.

Key Features & Types:

  • Type 1. Conventional PSPI: g, h, i-line UV sensitive. 2.38% TMAH development, Positive type. High resolution.
  • Type 2. ULK (Ultra Low-K) PSPI: High Photo-speed, 2.38% TMAH development, Positive type, Ultra Low-K (Dk=2.7 @1MHz).
  • Relatively high T%
  • Low water absorption
  • High thermal stability
  • Solar UV resistance

High Thermal Stability Polyimide Resin

These polyimide resins are designed for applications requiring exceptional thermal resistance and low coefficient of thermal expansion (CTE). They are crucial for flexible electronics and other high-temperature applications.

Key Properties (LTPI-01):

  • High Thermal Resistance: TD 5% [℃] = 576.96.
  • Low CTE: [ppm/℃] = 4.13.
  • Excellent Mechanical Property:
    • Tensile Strength [MPa] = 192.8
    • Modulus [GPa] = 7.9
  • Folding Endurance: Folding(MIT) TEST [cycle] > 150,000.
  • Smooth Surface: Suitable for high-precision applications.

Micro-lens Array (MLA)

Micro-lens arrays (MLAs) are used in OLED displays to enhance light extraction efficiency and improve brightness. These materials allow for the precise formation of micro-scale lens structures.

Key Features:

  • g, h, i-line UV sensitive.
  • 2.38% TMAH development, Negative type.
  • Round Shape MLA Pattern.
  • Enhancement OLED Brightness.
  • Relatively high T%.
  • Low-temperature cure.
  • Round shape pattern formation.

Semiconductor Fabrication

Bump Photoresist Remover

Specialized chemicals designed for the complete removal of photoresist materials used in the bumping process of semiconductor wafer fabrication. This process is critical for creating interconnects in advanced packaging technologies.

Key Features:

  • Single Spray & Dip Equipment Available.
  • Anti-corrosion for Metal (Au, Ti, Cu).
  • Remove completely Positive and Negative PR.

Target Applications

Wafer-level packaging, 3D IC packaging, and other advanced packaging techniques.

Via Etch Post-Etch Residue (PER) Remover

Formulated to clean and remove residues left behind after the via etching process in semiconductor manufacturing. Ensures clean and reliable via connections.

Key Features:

  • Single Spray & Dip Equipment Available.
  • Anti-corrosion for Metal (Au, Ti, Cu, Al).
  • Remove completely PER in VIA Hole.

Target Applications

Via cleaning in integrated circuit fabrication.

Negative Photo-sensitive Polyimide (for Semiconductor)

A specialized polyimide material used as a dielectric layer in semiconductor devices. It offers excellent thermal and chemical stability, and can be patterned using photolithography.

Key Features:

  • i-line UV Patternable.
  • Solvent developable negative type PSPI.
  • Low-temperature cure (200℃).
  • Tg = 206℃ measured by DSC.
  • TGA 5wt% loss Temp. = 321℃.

Target Applications

Dielectric layers, passivation layers, and stress buffer layers in semiconductor devices.

Ceramic Materials

SOFC Solid Electrolyte 8-YSZ

8-YSZ (8 mol% Yttria-Stabilized Zirconia) is a high-performance ceramic material used as a solid electrolyte in Solid Oxide Fuel Cells (SOFCs). It exhibits high ionic conductivity and stability at elevated temperatures.

Key Features & Properties (8-YSZ - C):

  • Appearance: White powder.
  • BET: 7±2 m²/g.
  • Granule size D50 μm: 50-70 μm.
  • Loss on ignition: 1.5% max.
  • Chemical Composition:
    • ZrO₂ + HfO₂ + Y₂O₃ + Al₂O₃: min. 99.9 wt%
    • Y₂O₃: 13.3 wt%
    • Al₂O₃, SiO₂, Fe₂O₃, TiO₂, Na₂O: 0.01% max (each)
  • Mechanical Properties:
    • Bending Strength: 1000-1200 MPa.
    • Hardness (Hv10): 1250.

Target Applications

Solid Oxide Fuel Cells (SOFCs) for power generation.

SOFC Metal Separator Development

Metal separators are key components in SOFC stacks, providing structural support, gas separation, and electrical conductivity. Advanced metal separators are designed for high performance and durability in the demanding SOFC operating environment.

Key Features:

  • High electrical conductivity.
  • Low gas permeability.
  • Stability in oxidizing/reducing atmospheres.
  • Similar thermal expansion coefficient to other cell components.
  • High mechanical strength and flatness at high temperatures.
  • Easy to form and process.

Target Applications

Solid Oxide Fuel Cells (SOFCs) for power generation, particularly in high-temperature applications.
