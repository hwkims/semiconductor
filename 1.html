Advanced Chemical Solutions for
FPD & Semiconductor Manufacturing

This document delves into state-of-the-art chemical materials used in the fabrication of advanced displays (FPD) and semiconductors. We explore the underlying science, focusing on their performance attributes, practical uses, and key benefits.

Display Technologies

🧪

Aqueous Strippers

Aqueous strippers are an environmentally conscious alternative to traditional solvent-based solutions in display manufacturing. They effectively remove photoresist and other polymeric residues without damaging underlying layers.

Target Applications

Primarily used in TFT-LCD and OLED display manufacturing for removing photoresist after patterning. Also applicable for cleaning photomasks and other components.

Key Advantages of Aqueous Strippers:

  • Environmentally Friendly: Reduced VOC emissions.
  • High Efficiency: Complete removal of various photoresist types.
  • Material Compatibility: Compatible with glass, ITO, and metals.
  • Cost-Effective: Lower overall process cost due to reduced waste.
  • Safety: Improved worker safety due to reduced flammability.
💡

LTOC (Low-Temperature Organic Compound) for OLED

LTOC materials are specialized organic compounds for OLED display fabrication. They form thin-film layers with specific optical and electrical properties, enabling high-performance OLED devices.

Key Properties & Performance Metrics

  • High Transparency: 98% transmission (T%) at 400nm.
  • Precise Resolution: Achieves 10µm Line & Space (L&S).
  • Rapid Photo-Speed: 90mJ/cm² photo-speed.
  • Controlled Mask C.D.: Precise control over critical dimensions.
  • Optimized Taper Angle: Ensures proper layer formation.
  • Well-Defined Contact Holes: For reliable connections.

Target Applications

Touch Panel for Flexible OLED, Low-temperature curing (below 100℃), OLED Pixel Defined Layer (PDL).

⚫

Black Resist for OLED

Black resist materials are essential for high-contrast OLED displays, acting as light-blocking layers to prevent leakage and improve image quality.

Key Properties:

  • High Optical Density (O.D): O.D = 1.0/µm.
  • Standard Developer Compatibility: 2.38% TMAH Development.
  • Fine Resolution: 14µm Hole resolution.
  • Two Types: Cardo-based and Siloxane-based.

Target Applications

Light blocking, Anti-reflection, Pixel Define Layer, R, G, B Pattern.

🛡️

Photo-sensitive Polyimide (PSPI)

PSPIs are advanced materials used in microelectronics for their thermal stability, chemical resistance, and dielectric properties, combining polyimide benefits with photolithographic patterning.

Key Features & Types:

  • Type 1. Conventional PSPI: g, h, i-line UV sensitive. 2.38% TMAH development, Positive type. High resolution.
  • Type 2. ULK (Ultra Low-K) PSPI: High Photo-speed, 2.38% TMAH development, Positive type, Ultra Low-K (Dk=2.7 @1MHz).
  • Relatively high T%
  • Low water absorption
  • High thermal stability
  • Solar UV resistance
🔥

High Thermal Stability Polyimide Resin

These resins are designed for applications requiring exceptional thermal resistance and low CTE, crucial for flexible electronics and high-temperature uses.

Key Properties (LTPI-01):

  • High Thermal Resistance: TD 5% [℃] = 576.96.
  • Low CTE: [ppm/℃] = 4.13.
  • Excellent Mechanical Property:
    • Tensile Strength [MPa] = 192.8
    • Modulus [GPa] = 7.9
  • Folding Endurance: Folding(MIT) TEST [cycle] > 150,000.
  • Smooth Surface: Suitable for high-precision applications.
🔆

Micro-lens Array (MLA)

MLAs enhance light extraction and brightness in OLED displays. These materials enable precise formation of micro-scale lens structures.

Key Features:

  • g, h, i-line UV sensitive.
  • 2.38% TMAH development, Negative type.
  • Round Shape MLA Pattern.
  • Enhancement OLED Brightness.
  • Relatively high T%.
  • Low-temperature cure.
  • Round shape pattern formation.

Semiconductor Fabrication

Bump Photoresist Remover

Specialized chemicals designed for complete removal of photoresist in wafer bumping, critical for interconnects in advanced packaging.

Key Features:

  • Single Spray & Dip Equipment Available.
  • Anti-corrosion for Metal (Au, Ti, Cu).
  • Remove completely Positive and Negative PR.

Target Applications

Wafer-level packaging, 3D IC packaging, and other advanced packaging.

💧

Via Etch Post-Etch Residue (PER) Remover

Formulated to clean residues after via etching, ensuring clean and reliable via connections.

Key Features:

  • Single Spray & Dip Equipment Available.
  • Anti-corrosion for Metal (Au, Ti, Cu, Al).
  • Remove completely PER in VIA Hole.

Target Applications

Via cleaning in integrated circuit fabrication.

🧱

Negative Photo-sensitive Polyimide (for Semiconductor)

A polyimide used as a dielectric layer, offering thermal and chemical stability, and patternable via photolithography.

Key Features:

  • i-line UV Patternable.
  • Solvent developable negative type PSPI.
  • Low-temperature cure (200℃).
  • Tg = 206℃ (measured by DSC).
  • TGA 5wt% loss Temp. = 321℃.

Target Applications

Dielectric layers, passivation layers, and stress buffer layers.

Ceramic Materials

⚪

SOFC Solid Electrolyte 8-YSZ

8-YSZ (8 mol% Yttria-Stabilized Zirconia) is a high-performance ceramic used as a solid electrolyte in SOFCs, with high ionic conductivity and stability.

Key Features & Properties (8-YSZ - C):

  • Appearance: White powder.
  • BET: 7±2 m²/g.
  • Granule size D50 μm: 50-70 μm.
  • Loss on ignition: 1.5% max.
  • Chemical Composition:
    • ZrO₂ + HfO₂ + Y₂O₃ + Al₂O₃: min. 99.9 wt%
    • Y₂O₃: 13.3 wt%
    • Al₂O₃, SiO₂, Fe₂O₃, TiO₂, Na₂O: 0.01% max (each)
  • Mechanical Properties:
    • Bending Strength: 1000-1200 MPa.
    • Hardness (Hv10): 1250.

Target Applications

Solid Oxide Fuel Cells (SOFCs) for power generation.

SOFC Metal Separator Development

Metal separators are key in SOFC stacks, providing support, gas separation, and electrical conductivity. They are designed for high performance and durability.

Key Features:

  • High electrical conductivity.
  • Low gas permeability.
  • Stability in oxidizing/reducing atmospheres.
  • Similar thermal expansion coefficient to cell components.
  • High mechanical strength and flatness at high temperatures.
  • Easy to form and process.

Target Applications

Solid Oxide Fuel Cells (SOFCs), especially for high-temperature use.
